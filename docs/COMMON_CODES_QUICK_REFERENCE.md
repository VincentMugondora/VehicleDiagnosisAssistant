# Quick Reference - Most Common OBD Codes

This is a quick reference card for the 20 most common OBD codes you'll encounter when testing with real users.

## Top 20 Most Common Codes

### 1. P0420 - Catalytic Converter Efficiency [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Catalyst not working efficiently  
**Common cause**: Worn out catalytic converter  
**Quick check**: Test O2 sensors first (cheaper than cat replacement)  
**User sees**: Check engine light, may fail emissions

---

### 2. P0442 - Small EVAP Leak [LOW]
**What it means**: Small leak in fuel vapor system  
**Common cause**: Loose or damaged gas cap (90% of cases!)  
**Quick check**: Tighten or replace gas cap, clear code  
**User sees**: Check engine light, maybe fuel smell

---

### 3. P0171 - System Too Lean (Bank 1) [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Engine getting too much air or not enough fuel  
**Common cause**: Vacuum leak, dirty MAF sensor  
**Quick check**: Look for vacuum leaks, check MAF  
**User sees**: Rough idle, poor acceleration, hesitation

---

### 4. P0300 - Random Misfire [HIGH]
**What it means**: Multiple cylinders misfiring  
**Common cause**: Bad spark plugs or ignition coils  
**Quick check**: Check spark plugs first  
**User sees**: Rough idle, loss of power, shaking, flashing check engine light

---

### 5. P0172 - System Too Rich (Bank 1) [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Engine getting too much fuel  
**Common cause**: Faulty O2 sensor, leaking fuel injectors  
**Quick check**: Check O2 sensors, look for black smoke  
**User sees**: Poor fuel economy, black exhaust, strong fuel smell

---

### 6. P0301-P0308 - Cylinder Misfire [HIGH]
**What it means**: Specific cylinder not firing properly  
**Common cause**: Bad spark plug or ignition coil on that cylinder  
**Quick check**: Swap spark plug/coil to another cylinder  
**User sees**: Rough idle, power loss, flashing check engine light

---

### 7. P0101 - MAF Sensor Range/Performance [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Mass Air Flow sensor reading wrong  
**Common cause**: Dirty MAF sensor  
**Quick check**: Clean MAF sensor with MAF cleaner  
**User sees**: Poor acceleration, rough idle, stalling

---

### 8. P0455 - Large EVAP Leak [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Large leak in fuel vapor system  
**Common cause**: Loose/missing gas cap, cracked EVAP hose  
**Quick check**: Check gas cap, inspect EVAP hoses  
**User sees**: Check engine light, strong fuel smell

---

### 9. P0401 - EGR Flow Insufficient [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Exhaust gas recirculation not flowing enough  
**Common cause**: Clogged EGR valve (carbon buildup)  
**Quick check**: Clean EGR valve and passages  
**User sees**: Failed emissions, rough idle, hesitation

---

### 10. P0128 - Coolant Temperature Below Thermostat [LOW]
**What it means**: Engine not warming up properly  
**Common cause**: Stuck open thermostat  
**Quick check**: Replace thermostat  
**User sees**: Slow warm-up, poor heater performance, poor fuel economy

---

### 11. P0340 - Camshaft Position Sensor [HIGH]
**What it means**: Camshaft sensor not working  
**Common cause**: Failed cam sensor, timing chain issue  
**Quick check**: Test cam sensor, check timing  
**User sees**: Hard starting, rough idle, stalling

---

### 12. P0335 - Crankshaft Position Sensor [HIGH]
**What it means**: Crankshaft sensor not working  
**Common cause**: Failed crank sensor, damaged wiring  
**Quick check**: Test crank sensor, check wiring  
**User sees**: No start, intermittent stalling

---

### 13. P0430 - Catalyst Efficiency Bank 2 [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Second catalytic converter not efficient (V6/V8)  
**Common cause**: Worn out catalytic converter  
**Quick check**: Test O2 sensors first  
**User sees**: Check engine light, failed emissions

---

### 14. P0440 - EVAP System Malfunction [LOW]
**What it means**: General EVAP system problem  
**Common cause**: Loose gas cap, faulty purge valve  
**Quick check**: Tighten gas cap, test purge valve  
**User sees**: Check engine light, possible fuel smell

---

### 15. P0507 - Idle Speed Higher Than Expected [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Engine idling too high  
**Common cause**: Vacuum leak, stuck IAC valve  
**Quick check**: Look for vacuum leaks, clean throttle body  
**User sees**: High idle RPM, racing engine

---

### 16. P0506 - Idle Speed Lower Than Expected [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Engine idling too low  
**Common cause**: Dirty throttle body, vacuum leak  
**Quick check**: Clean throttle body, check for leaks  
**User sees**: Low idle, stalling, rough idle

---

### 17. P0700 - Transmission Control System [HIGH]
**What it means**: General transmission problem (scan for more codes)  
**Common cause**: Internal transmission issue, solenoid failure  
**Quick check**: Check transmission fluid, scan for P07xx codes  
**User sees**: Transmission problems, shifting issues, may go into limp mode

---

### 18. P0133 - O2 Sensor Slow Response [MEDIUM]
**What it means**: Upstream O2 sensor responding slowly  
**Common cause**: Aged or contaminated O2 sensor  
**Quick check**: Replace O2 sensor  
**User sees**: Poor fuel economy, failed emissions

---

### 19. P0125 - Insufficient Coolant Temperature [LOW]
**What it means**: Engine taking too long to warm up  
**Common cause**: Faulty thermostat, coolant temp sensor  
**Quick check**: Test thermostat, check coolant level  
**User sees**: Slow warm-up, poor heater, check engine light

---

### 20. P0441 - EVAP Purge Flow [LOW]
**What it means**: EVAP purge valve not working right  
**Common cause**: Faulty purge valve, blocked canister  
**Quick check**: Test purge valve operation  
**User sees**: Check engine light, possible rough idle

---

## Quick Diagnosis Tips

### First Steps for Any Code
1. ✅ **Read the exact code** - Don't guess from symptoms
2. ✅ **Check for multiple codes** - Related codes tell a story
3. ✅ **Note when it appeared** - After what event/service?
4. ✅ **Check easy fixes first** - Gas cap, fluid levels, connections

### By Severity

#### HIGH - Stop Driving / Fix ASAP
- Flashing check engine light = STOP (P0300-P0308)
- Overheating (P0217)
- No start (P0335, P0340)
- Transmission issues (P0700)
- Airbag lights (B0001-B0032)

#### MEDIUM - Drive Carefully / Fix Soon
- Steady check engine light
- Catalyst codes (P0420, P0430)
- O2 sensors (P0130-P0141)
- Fuel trim (P0171, P0172)
- MAF issues (P0101)

#### LOW - OK to Drive / Fix at Service
- EVAP codes (P0442, P0456, P0440)
- Thermostat (P0125, P0128)
- Minor sensor issues

### Quick Fixes to Try First

**P0442, P0455, P0456 (EVAP leaks)**
→ Tighten gas cap, drive 50 miles, see if code clears

**P0101 (MAF sensor)**
→ Clean MAF sensor with MAF cleaner spray

**P0171, P0172 (Fuel trim)**
→ Check for vacuum leaks (listen for hissing)

**P0300-P0308 (Misfires)**
→ Check/replace spark plugs first

**P0401 (EGR)**
→ Clean EGR valve with carburetor cleaner

**P0506, P0507 (Idle control)**
→ Clean throttle body

**P0420, P0430 (Catalyst)**
→ Test O2 sensors before buying expensive cat

### Warning Signs to Watch For

🚨 **Flashing Check Engine Light**
- = Active misfire damaging catalyst
- Pull over safely, call for tow
- Don't drive!

🔴 **Red Warning Lights**
- Oil pressure low
- Engine temperature high
- Stop immediately!

🟡 **Yellow Check Engine Light**
- = Emissions/performance issue
- OK to drive carefully
- Get checked soon

## Code Combinations to Note

**P0171 + P0174** = Vacuum leak affecting both banks  
**P0300 + P0171** = Vacuum leak causing misfires  
**P0420 + P0430** = Both catalysts (expensive!)  
**P0301-P0304 (sequential)** = Probably ignition coil pack  
**P0171 + P0101** = Probably MAF sensor issue  

## Testing with Real Users - What to Expect

### Most Common User Scenarios (80%)
1. "My check engine light is on" → P0420, P0442
2. "Car running rough" → P0300-P0308, P0171
3. "Hard to start" → P0335, P0340
4. "Gas cap message" → P0442, P0455
5. "Failed emissions test" → P0420, P0401, P0133

### Less Common (15%)
6. "Transmission acting up" → P0700-P0765
7. "Idle is weird" → P0506, P0507
8. "Poor fuel economy" → P0171, P0172, P0101
9. "No heat" → P0125, P0128

### Rare (5%)
10. Network/electrical issues → U0100-U0164, B-codes

## Pro Tips

### For P0420/P0430 (Catalyst)
- Test O2 sensors FIRST (cheaper)
- Check for exhaust leaks
- Could be false alarm from bad sensor
- Expensive repair if really the cat

### For P0442 (Small EVAP Leak)
- 90% of the time = loose gas cap
- Tell user to tighten cap, drive 50 miles
- If returns, then inspect EVAP system
- Don't replace parts until gas cap tried

### For P0300-P0308 (Misfire)
- Flashing light = SERIOUS
- Start with spark plugs (cheapest)
- Then ignition coils
- Then fuel injectors
- Could also be vacuum leak or compression

### For P0171/P0172 (Fuel Trim)
- Look for vacuum leaks first
- Check MAF sensor
- Test fuel pressure
- Inspect O2 sensors
- Could be dirty injectors

## Remember

✅ **Always start simple** - Gas cap, fluids, connections  
✅ **Check multiple codes** - They tell a story together  
✅ **Test cheap parts first** - Spark plugs before coils  
✅ **Verify before replacing** - Test sensors, don't guess  
✅ **Severity matters** - High = urgent, Low = can wait
